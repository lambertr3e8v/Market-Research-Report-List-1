<p><p>The electrophysiology diagnostic catheters market is divided into two main types: imaging catheters and non-imaging catheters. Imaging catheters are equipped with advanced visualization technology, allowing for real-time imaging of cardiac structures and electrophysiological activity, enhancing procedural accuracy and patient outcomes. Non-imaging catheters, on the other hand, are primarily used for electrical mapping and stimulation without visual feedback, focusing on recording electrical signals from the heart. Together, these catheters play a crucial role in diagnosing and treating cardiac arrhythmias.</p></p>
