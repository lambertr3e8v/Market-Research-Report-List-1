<p><p>The vascular prosthesis market includes several types of grafts used for surgical procedures. Polyester grafts are widely utilized for their strength and biocompatibility. ePTFE (expanded polytetrafluoroethylene) grafts offer excellent resistance to thrombosis and are used in both arterial and venous applications. Polyurethane grafts provide flexibility and durability, making them suitable for dynamic environments. Biosynthetic grafts combine synthetic and biological materials, promoting better integration with surrounding tissues and reducing complications, thus gaining popularity in vascular interventions.</p></p>
