<p><p>The automotive AWD (All-Wheel Drive) systems market is categorized into two primary types: automatic AWD and manual AWD. Automatic AWD systems engage and disengage power to all wheels as needed, enhancing traction and handling without driver input, making them ideal for varied driving conditions. In contrast, manual AWD systems require driver intervention to activate all-wheel drive, offering more control over terrain and driving experience, often preferred by enthusiasts or in off-road scenarios. Both systems cater to different consumer needs in the automotive market.</p></p>
