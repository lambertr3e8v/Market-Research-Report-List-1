<p><p>The pruritus drugs market is categorized into three main types: topical, oral, and parenteral. Topical drugs are often creams and ointments applied directly to the skin to provide localized relief from itchiness. Oral medications, including antihistamines and corticosteroids, are taken by mouth to address systemic causes of pruritus. Parenteral drugs involve injections or infusions for rapid onset of action, typically used in more severe cases where immediate relief is needed. Each type serves different patient needs and treatment scenarios.</p></p>
