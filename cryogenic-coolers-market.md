<p><p>The cryogenic coolers market comprises several types, each with unique operating principles. Gifford-McMahon cryocoolers utilize a refrigerant gas cycle for cooling. Pulse-tube cryocoolers employ oscillating gas flow for efficient refrigeration. Stirling cryocoolers operate based on the Stirling cycle, offering high efficiency and reliability. Joule-Thomson cryocoolers achieve cooling through gas expansion. Brayton cryocoolers are based on the Brayton cycle, useful in specific applications. Other market types may include custom or hybrid systems tailored for niche requirements in cryogenic applications.</p></p>
