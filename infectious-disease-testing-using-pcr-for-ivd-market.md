<p><p>Infectious disease testing using PCR for in vitro diagnostics (IVD) encompasses two main market types: direct detection and indirect detection. Direct detection involves identifying pathogens by amplifying their genetic material, allowing for rapid and specific diagnosis of infections. In contrast, indirect detection focuses on the immune response to pathogens, using PCR to identify antibodies or other markers resulting from an infection. Both approaches enhance diagnostic accuracy, enabling timely treatment decisions and improving patient outcomes in infectious disease management.</p></p>
