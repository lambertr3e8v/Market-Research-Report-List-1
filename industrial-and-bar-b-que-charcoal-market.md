<p><p>The industrial and barbecue charcoal market consists of various types, each suited for specific uses. Lump charcoal is made from whole wood pieces, offering a natural burn. Japanese charcoal includes black charcoal, known for high heat and low smoke, and white charcoal, recognized for its purity. Extruded charcoal is formed under pressure, ensuring uniformity. Charcoal briquettes, often used for grilling, combine charcoal dust with binders. Sugar charcoal, derived from sugarcane, provides a unique flavor and is popular in certain culinary applications.</p></p>
